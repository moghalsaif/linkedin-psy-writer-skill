# LinkedIn Psychology Writer

Write LinkedIn posts that trigger emotional responses, not just deliver information.
Based on the Paolo Trivellato emotional architecture framework + WhyVanamali writing system.

**Core truth:** The algorithm rewards emotional intensity. Your job is not to inform — it is to make someone feel something powerful enough that they can't scroll past.

---

## BANNED PATTERNS — Never Write These

These are the fingerprints of AI-generated content. Sophisticated readers spot them instantly and disengage. Every sentence in every post must pass this filter.

### Banned sentence constructions:

**The "Not X, it's Y" pivot**
- BAD: "The tool didn't fail them. The implementation did."
- BAD: "This isn't a content problem. It's a psychology problem."
- WHY: Sounds like a PowerPoint slide. Over-used structure that reads as manufactured insight.

**The "This is why / That's why / Here's why" explainer**
- BAD: "This is why most founders never book inbound calls."
- BAD: "That's why the algorithm rewards emotion over information."
- WHY: Reads like a teacher explaining to a student. Kills the peer-to-peer voice.

**The "Not because of X, but because of Y" construction**
- BAD: "Not because AI doesn't work, but because nobody told you implementation takes 11 steps."
- BAD: "Not because your offer is bad, but because your positioning is off."
- WHY: Overused AI sentence scaffold. Immediately signals generated text.

**The "The difference is..." pivot**
- BAD: "The difference isn't the value. It's the psychology."
- BAD: "The difference between them? One optimizes for information, the other for emotion."
- WHY: Every AI-written LinkedIn post uses this. It's a cliche now.

**The "And that's X" closer**
- BAD: "And that's the real problem."
- BAD: "And that's what nobody tells you."
- WHY: Lazy emphasis. If you need to announce that something is the point, rewrite it so it's obviously the point.

**The "Most people X. But Y people Z" setup**
- BAD: "Most founders obsess over tools. But the best operators obsess over workflows."
- WHY: Overused contrast structure. Every thought leader template uses this exact frame.

**The "Here's the truth:" / "Here's the thing:" setup**
- BAD: "Here's the truth: your audience doesn't care about your process."
- WHY: Signals a manufactured insight is about to follow. Readers skip past it.

### What to do instead:

Instead of announcing the insight, embed it in a specific observation.

- BAD: "The difference is he triggers emotions and you deliver information."
- GOOD: "He posted 3 times a week and booked 20 calls. You posted every day and booked zero. Same audience. Same industry. He wrote like he was angry about something. You wrote like you were teaching a class."

Instead of the pivot structure, show the contrast through two concrete scenes.

- BAD: "This isn't a content problem. It's a positioning problem."
- GOOD: "You moved to Dubai. Updated your LinkedIn headline. Posted excited to announce. Then spent 12 months chasing the same clients at the same rates you charged in Hyderabad."

**The rule: Show the gap. Don't name it.**

## Step 0: Identify Post Type

Ask the user (or infer from context) which mode applies:

| Mode | When to use |
|------|-------------|
| **A. Standard Engagement Post** | Building audience, thought leadership, general content |
| **B. Lead Magnet Post** | Offering a resource in exchange for a comment/DM |
| **C. TOF Belief Disruption** | Targeting cold/unaware audiences; shifting a belief they currently hold |

If unclear, ask one question: *"Is this post meant to build reach, capture leads, or challenge how someone currently thinks?"*

---

## Step 1: Choose Emotional Triggers

Every post must be built around **1 primary trigger** and **1 secondary trigger**. Trying to hit all 6 creates confusing, unfocused content.

### The 6 Triggers (reference: [triggers.md](references/triggers.md) for full detail)

| # | Trigger | What it creates | Best used when... |
|---|---------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 1 | **Identity Validation** | "You GET me" — rush of recognition | You know an uncomfortable truth your audience secretly believes |
| 2 | **Status Signaling** | "Sharing this makes me look good" — drives shares | You have insider knowledge, data, or a contrarian take worth broadcasting |
| 3 | **Tribal Belonging** | "I'm one of THEM, not THEM" — in-group defense | You can clearly define a sophisticated "us" vs naive "them" |
| 4 | **Productive Discomfort** | "I need to fix this" — cognitive dissonance | You can expose a gap between their self-image and current behavior |
| 5 | **Curiosity Gap** | "I need to know what this is" — compulsive reading | You have a specific outcome/framework/result to tease |
| 6 | **Aspiration & Possibility** | "I could do that" — emotional investment in outcome | You have proof of an achievable result (not a fantasy claim) |

**Pick your pair, then proceed.**

---

## Step 2: Write the Hook

The hook is lines 1-3. It must trigger the primary emotion immediately.

### Hook formula by trigger:

**Identity Validation hook:**
> State the uncomfortable truth they know but don't say.
> "You're not struggling to find clients because of your offer. You're scared to charge what you're worth."

**Status Signaling hook:**
> Lead with insider knowledge or contrarian data.
> "Most agencies tell you more content = more results. Here's what the data actually shows."

**Tribal Belonging hook:**
> Draw the line between "us" and "them" immediately.
> "There are two types of founders: ones who obsess over their follower count, and ones who obsess over their bank account."

**Productive Discomfort hook:**
> Open with the uncomfortable truth about their behavior.
> "You've posted on LinkedIn for 6 months. Your inbound calls from it? Zero."

**Curiosity Gap hook:**
> Specific outcome + implied framework + tension.
> "The post structure that booked me 47 calls in 72 hours (copy the exact framework)."
> Elements: specific number, specific timeframe, promised payoff.

**Aspiration hook:**
> Impressive but achievable result + accessible method.
> "How I went from 0 to $42k MRR in 90 days using only LinkedIn."
> NOT: "How I made $10M overnight." (triggers skepticism, not aspiration)

### Hook rules (always apply):
- Bold claim first. No preamble. No context-setting. No "Hi LinkedIn fam."
- Line 1 = the punch. Line 2 = why it matters to the reader. Line 3 = evidence/proof.
- Use specifics: numbers, names, timeframes. Vague = invisible.
- If it wouldn't stop a scroll, rewrite it.

---

## Step 3: Build the Body

The body deepens engagement using the **secondary trigger**.

### Body pairings that work:

| Primary Hook | Best Secondary Body |
|---|---|
| Curiosity Gap | → Aspiration (show what's possible when gap is closed) |
| Productive Discomfort | → Identity Validation (it's not your fault — here's why) |
| Tribal Belonging | → Status Signaling (here's what the sophisticated "us" knows) |
| Identity Validation | → Aspiration (here's what's possible once you see this) |
| Status Signaling | → Curiosity Gap (here's the framework behind the insight) |

### Body structure:
```
[Expand the problem/situation — 2-4 short paragraphs]
[One sharp observation that reframes the situation]
[Transition: "The difference is..." / "Here's what actually works..." / "What they don't tell you is..."]
[The insight / the payoff / the reframe]
```

**Rhythm rules (from WhyVanamali):**
- Short sentence. Then a longer one that expands the thought.
- First person: "I noticed", "I tested", "Here's what I found"
- Reader as participant: "You've seen this", "You know this feeling"
- Discovery tone — not expert-dispensing-knowledge tone.

---

## Step 4: Close with Action

Every post ends with something the reader can DO.

| Post Type | Closing move |
|---|---|
| Engagement post | Ask a question that invites a real answer: "What's been your experience with this?" |
| Lead magnet | Comment trigger: "Comment 'WORD' and I'll send it over." |
| Belief disruption | Reframe they can apply immediately: "Try this next time you..." |

---

## Step 5: If Lead Magnet — Stack the FOMO

Lead magnets need stronger emotional triggers because there's friction (asking for a comment/DM).

Add all 4 FOMO layers in the post body:

```
1. Social proof FOMO: "I've sent this to 847 people this month and the feedback has been wild."
2. Transformation FOMO: "One founder used this and booked 23 calls in a week."
3. Exclusivity signal: "I normally only share this with $3k/month clients. Testing something."
4. Timing urgency: "Comment below — I'll respond within a few hours if this post gets traction."
```

Also apply **Investment Signaling** — prove effort, don't just promise value:
- "The 43-page playbook I built over 18 months with 32 B2B companies" > "Free guide to X"
- "The exact swipe file of 156 hooks that generated 2M+ impressions" > "Hook templates"
- "The 3-hour video I recorded for my $15k clients" > "Free video walkthrough"

## Step 6: If TOF Belief Disruption — Follow the Structure

TOF ragebait is **not** controversy for attention. It is belief disruption — taking something your audience currently believes and showing why it's wrong.

### The 5-step belief disruption structure:

```
1. STATE the common belief clearly
   "Most founders think the way to grow on LinkedIn is to post valuable content consistently."

2. CREATE DOUBT with contradicting evidence
   "But I know founders who've posted 5 days a week for a year with under 1,000 followers."

3. INTRODUCE the alternative frame
   "The difference isn't the value. It's the psychology. One group delivers information.
   The other triggers emotional response. LinkedIn only rewards one of those."

4. SHOW the implication
   "Which means most founders are working 5x harder for 10x worse results."

5. OFFER the path forward (optional — use for lead magnets)
   "Comment 'PSYCH' and I'll send the full breakdown."
```

The goal: by the end of this post, a **problem-unaware** person has become **problem-aware**.

## Step 7: Authenticity Filter (Run Before Every Post)

Emotional content only works when it's grounded. Sophisticated audiences detect manipulation instantly.

Before finalizing, check:

- [ ] **Is this true?** Not "could be true" — is it true from YOUR direct experience?
- [ ] **Would you say this at dinner?** If it would feel try-hard in person, it reads that way on LinkedIn.
- [ ] **Is the emotion proportional?** Extreme language for a minor point = guru energy.
- [ ] **Does it serve the reader or you?** If you're triggering emotion to get engagement rather than to help — it will show.
- [ ] **Is it specific enough?** "Most people get this wrong" is generic. "B2B founders charging under $5k/month who've posted 6+ months without one inbound call" is specific.
- [ ] **Do you have proof?** Extraordinary claims need screenshots, case studies, real numbers. Proof transforms hype into believable aspiration.
